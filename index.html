Project Scope

Domain Overview

Mental health disorders affect 1 in 8 people globally. This research builds a proactive, AI-powered system that closes the gap between data-driven prediction and real-world intervention.

🧠

CBT Chatbot

A Cognitive Behavioral Therapy-based conversational companion available 24/7. Built with Rasa, it assesses emotional state, guides breathing exercises, and provides reflection prompts tailored to each user.

👤

Digital Twin

A real-time dynamic model of the user's emotional state, classifying them as Stable, Monitoring, At Risk, or Action Required. Delivers personalized recommendations via deterministic and collaborative filtering.

📊

Mood Dashboard

Visual summaries of emotional trends — daily, weekly, and monthly — with downloadable PDF reports. Enables users and clinicians to track progress and identify patterns over time.

🔍

Sentiment Analysis

Fine-tuned BERT transformer models classify emotional states from text, while EfficientNet-B0 CNN models analyze facial expressions from images, providing multimodal emotional intelligence.

🔐

Privacy-First Design

End-to-end encryption, multi-factor authentication, role-based access control, and user-controlled consent management ensure sensitive mental health data is always protected.

📱

Cross-Platform App

Built in Flutter (Dart) for both Android and iOS, backed by FastAPI on Google Cloud. Firebase handles authentication and push notifications for timely interventions.

Domain · Literature Survey

What Research Tells Us

A growing body of evidence demonstrates that social media data can reliably predict mental health conditions — often months before clinical diagnosis.

Condition Platform / Data Method Key Finding
Depression & PTSD Twitter ML — linguistic & temporal signals Predicted months before clinical diagnosis (Reece et al., 2016)
Depression Twitter Social media models ~70% accuracy before official diagnosis (UW / Microsoft Research)
Suicidal Ideation Social posts Ensemble LSTM–Attention–CNN 90.3% accuracy, 92.6% F1 score (Renjith et al., 2021)
Anxiety Instagram, Facebook Sentiment analysis + behavioral markers Elevated anxiety symptoms correlated with platform use frequency
Anorexia / BDD Selfies + posts Body image content filtering Exposure to idealized content identified as a key trigger
Bipolar / OCD-like Social media content Exploratory digital analysis Compulsive use patterns correlated with elevated bipolar-like behavior

Domain · Research Gap

Where Existing Solutions Fall Short

While individual components — chatbots, digital twins, dashboards — exist in isolation, no integrated system combines all three into one cohesive, privacy-conscious platform.

  • 1

    Siloed Architecture

    Existing systems use a siloed approach — CBT chatbots, digital twins, and dashboards are developed independently with no real-time data exchange between components. This fragmentation severely limits personalization and adaptive intervention.

  • 2

    Fragmented User Experience

    Users must navigate multiple applications to access mental health support, leading to disengagement. Each context switch breaks therapeutic continuity and undermines trust in the system.

  • 3

    Reactive Rather Than Proactive

    Current approaches rely on self-disclosure and periodic clinical appointments. There is no mechanism to continuously monitor emotional trajectory and intervene before a crisis occurs.

  • 4

    Inadequate Privacy & Security

    Sensitive mental health data flows across multiple platforms, each with limited security boundaries. Comprehensive end-to-end encryption, MFA, and role-based access control are largely absent in current solutions.

  • 5

    Limited Multimodal Intelligence

    Most studies rely on text alone. Combining facial-expression analysis (images) with natural language sentiment analysis provides a far richer and more accurate picture of mental state that remains underexplored.

Domain · Research Problem & Solution

The Problem We're Solving

"How can social media activity and sentiment analysis be used to proactively predict mental health conditions and deliver personalized, real-time intervention?"

Mental health assessment today depends almost entirely on self-reporting and infrequent clinical sessions. There is no longitudinal, continuous system that tracks behavioral and emotional signals from daily digital activity — social media text, images, and interaction patterns — and translates them into timely, personalized guidance. The result is delayed diagnosis, missed early warning signs, and poor access to evidence-based support.

💡

Proposed Solution

An integrated mobile application that analyzes user-permitted social media text and images, predicts mental health risks, and delivers proactive support through a unified dashboard, digital twin avatar, and CBT-based chatbot — all within a single, secure platform.

🎯

Target Outcomes

Early detection of anxiety, depression, and stress markers. Personalized music therapy, mindfulness recommendations, and breathing exercises. Downloadable mental health progress reports. Real-time push notifications for at-risk users.

Domain · Research Objectives

What We Aim to Achieve

Five specific objectives guide the development of this integrated mental health prediction and intervention system.

01

Social Media Data Analysis

Study and implement methods to extract and analyze user-permitted social media data for behavioral and emotional insights using advanced NLP techniques.

02

Mood Dashboard

Design and develop a dashboard displaying mood trends, emotional history timelines, and downloadable mental health reports for users and clinicians.

03

Digital Twin Module

Create a digital twin that mirrors the user's mental state in real-time and provides personalized tips, reminders, and activity suggestions.

04

CBT Chatbot

Design a CBT-based chatbot for friendly, therapeutic conversation that alleviates anxiety and addresses mental health challenges through evidence-based dialogue flows.

05

Intervention Strategies

Study and integrate mental health indicators, coping strategies, and personalized intervention recommendations into the application's recommendation engine.

Domain · Methodology

How We're Building It

A full-stack architecture combining Flutter mobile, FastAPI backend, TensorFlow models, and Google Cloud infrastructure — containerized and privacy-by-design.

1

User Onboarding & Consent

OAuth-based social media authentication with explicit user consent management. All permissions stored as tokens; no data processed without active consent.

2

Data Ingestion & Preprocessing

Text and images ingested, anonymized, hashed, and streamed into analysis pipelines. Google Cloud Pub/Sub manages service communication with TLS encryption.

3

AI Emotion Analysis

Fine-tuned BERT models classify emotional states from text. EfficientNet-B0 CNN models analyze facial expressions from images. Models converted to TensorFlow Lite for on-device inference.

4

Dashboard & Reports

Syncfusion Flutter Charts render trend lines, distribution charts, and sentiment breakdowns. PDF reports generated via ReportLab and stored securely on Google Cloud Storage.

5

Digital Twin & Alerts

Rolling time-series scores classify users into four states. Collaborative filtering (ALS) surfaces personalized recommendations. Firebase Cloud Messaging sends push alerts for at-risk states.
