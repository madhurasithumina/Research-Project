📚 Literature Survey

Mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, and chronic stress affect millions globally. Traditional diagnosis often depends on clinical interviews and self-reporting, which can be delayed and inaccessible. Recent literature highlights that social media provides real-time, naturally occurring signals of emotional and psychological state.

Social media as a signal source

  • Textual patterns can reveal affect, cognition, and distress signals.
  • Language features (sentiment, pronouns, emotional tone) correlate with depression and anxiety.
  • Longer-form platforms can capture richer emotional narratives.

Deep learning improves prediction

  • Transformer models (e.g., BERT-like) capture context in informal language better than TF‑IDF/Word2Vec baselines.
  • Computer vision models can detect facial-expression patterns indicating emotional state.
  • Sequence models (LSTM/GRU) support trend analysis over time (sleep, stress, mood trajectories).

⚠️ Research Gap

Despite strong progress, current systems often struggle with real-world continuity, integration, and personalization.

Long-term tracking is limited

Many studies focus on one-time detection instead of monitoring changes over time.

Incomplete data integration

Most approaches rely on text or images or surveys, not a unified pipeline.

Platform constraints

Research frequently centers on Twitter/Reddit, underutilizing richer long-form content on platforms like Facebook.

Fusion conflicts

Multimodal systems can mis-handle mismatched signals (e.g., happy image with a distressed caption).

Little personalized intervention

Many works stop at prediction and do not deliver real-time support or recommendations.

Ethics and privacy

Consent, anonymization, and responsible AI practices are not consistently addressed.

❗ Research Problem & Proposed Solution

Mental health disorders are increasing worldwide, but early detection and continuous intervention are limited by delayed clinical diagnosis and fragmented understanding of behavior across data sources.

🔴 Research Problem

  • Diagnosis is often late and access is uneven.
  • Static, single-source models miss context and temporal patterns.
  • Social signals alone may be incomplete; surveys alone lack behavioral context.

🟢 Proposed Solution

  • Integrate social media text, images (facial cues), and survey data.
  • Model language with transformers; trends with LSTM/GRU; images with CNN backbones.
  • Fuse modalities into a single prediction with confidence-aware reasoning.
  • Deliver risk classification (low / moderate / high), trend monitoring, and personalized support.

🎯 Research Objectives

Main objective: build an intelligent, multimodal prediction and monitoring system enabling early detection and personalized intervention.

Specific objectives

  • Text-based NLP analysis for emotional patterns
  • Time-series prediction of changes over time (LSTM/GRU)
  • Multimodal text+image analysis for better robustness
  • Context-aware modeling for informal language
  • Risk classification (low / moderate / high)
  • CBT-inspired chatbot support
  • Dashboards + digital twin monitoring
  • Ethical implementation: privacy, consent, responsible AI

⚙️ Methodology

A structured pipeline from data collection to deployment and validation.

  1. 1. Data collection

    Facebook posts/captions, images, and survey signals (mood, sleep, stress, behavior).

  2. 2. Preprocessing

    Text cleaning, face detection/normalization, labeling, anonymization.

  3. 3. Feature extraction

    Sentiment and embeddings, facial emotion features, behavioral indicators.

  4. 4. Model development

    Transformers for text, CNN for images, LSTM/GRU for sequences, fusion for combined inference.

  5. 5. Prediction & classification

    Emotional state + risk level (low/moderate/high) with trend monitoring.

  6. 6–8. System + evaluation + deployment

    Dashboard, chatbot, digital twin; evaluate metrics; functional/usability/privacy testing.

✅ Final Summary

This research unifies four key components — text analysis, image analysis, time-series prediction, and behavioral signals — into a single monitoring system designed for practical, real-time support.

What the system delivers

  • Real-time risk estimation with transparent inputs
  • Long-term emotional trend analysis
  • Risk level classification: low, moderate, high
  • Personalized support via a CBT-inspired chatbot
  • Dashboards + digital twin for monitoring

Next steps (practical)

  1. Finalize data access, consent, and anonymization workflow
  2. Define labels and evaluation protocol
  3. Build baseline models then iterate to multimodal fusion
  4. Integrate dashboard + chatbot + monitoring view
